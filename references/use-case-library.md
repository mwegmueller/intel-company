# AI Use Case Library

A comprehensive library of AI use cases organized by industry and cross-industry applicability. This reference helps identify relevant AI opportunities for specific organizations based on their industry, business model, and strategic priorities.

---

## Cross-Industry Use Cases

These use cases apply across most industries and are strong candidates for consideration regardless of sector.

### 1. Intelligent Document Processing

**Description:** Automate extraction, classification, and processing of unstructured documents such as invoices, contracts, forms, and reports. Uses OCR, NLP, and machine learning to understand content and route it appropriately.

**Typical Business Area:** Operations, Finance, Legal, HR
**Typical Business Impact:** High
**Typical Complexity:** Medium
**Common Timeline:** 4-8 months
**Key Technologies:** OCR, NLP, document classification models
**Success Metrics:** Processing time reduction, error rate reduction, cost per document processed

---

### 2. Customer Service AI

**Typical Business Area:** Customer Service, Support, Sales
**Typical Business Impact:** High
**Typical Complexity:** Medium
**Common Timeline:** 3-6 months
**Key Technologies:** NLP, conversational AI, dialogue management

## Use Case Selection Criteria

When selecting the top 8 use cases for a company, evaluate each potential opportunity against these criteria. Higher scores across multiple criteria indicate stronger candidates.

### 1. Company-Specific Fit
**How well does the use case address a known or suspected pain point for this company?**

Evidence to look for:
- Mentions of the problem area in earnings calls, strategy documents, or annual reports
- References to manual processes or operational inefficiencies in this area
- Competitive disadvantages or challenges mentioned by management
- Customer feedback or complaints related to this area
- Cost or efficiency metrics suggesting problems

**Scoring:** High if multiple evidence points exist, Medium if some evidence, Low if no evidence

---

### 2. Industry Relevance
**Is this a proven use case pattern in their specific industry?**

Evidence to look for:
- Peer companies deploying similar AI solutions
- Industry analyst reports covering this use case
- Industry publications discussing the opportunity
- Number of companies in the industry addressing this problem
- Industry-specific regulations or standards affecting this area

**Scoring:** High if widespread adoption in industry, Medium if emerging pattern, Low if no industry precedent

---

### 3. Maturity Alignment
**Can the company realistically execute this use case given their current AI maturity?**

Guidelines:
- AI Leaders (8-10): Can tackle complex, cross-functional, novel use cases
- AI Advancing (6-7.9): Can execute well-defined, established use cases
- AI Developing (4-5.9): Should focus on simpler use cases with clear patterns
- AI Exploring (2-3.9): Need to start with low-risk, high-success-probability pilots
- AI Unaware (1-1.9): Should begin with short-term, high-ROI quick wins

**Scoring:** High if realistic given maturity, Medium if slightly stretched, Low if too ambitious

---

### 4. Strategic Priority
**Does the use case align with the company's stated business strategy?**

Evidence to look for:
- Explicit mention in strategy documents or annual reports
- CEO/leadership public statements about this priority area
- M&A or partnership activity related to this area
- Organizational changes suggesting strategic focus
- Budget allocation to related initiatives

**Scoring:** High if aligned with stated priorities, Medium if somewhat relevant, Low if misaligned

---

### 5. Impact Potential
**Will this use case meaningfully move the needle on key business metrics?**

Considerations:
- Revenue impact (new revenue vs. cost reduction)
- Cost savings potential (percentage of controllable costs)
- Customer impact (satisfaction, retention, lifetime value)
- Operational impact (efficiency, speed, quality)
- Market share or competitive positioning benefit

**Scoring:** High if 5%+ potential impact on key metrics, Medium if 1-5%, Low if <1%

---

### 6. Differentiation Potential
**Does this use case help the company compete better or create unique advantages?**

Evidence to look for:
- Capability gaps vs. competitors
- Unique assets the company has (data, domain expertise, customer relationships)
- Potential to create sustainable competitive advantage
- Market barriers the company could establish
- Patent or proprietary approach potential

**Scoring:** High if significant differentiation potential, Medium if some advantage, Low if commodity use case

---

### 7. Data Availability
**Does the company likely have the necessary data to support this use case?**

Evidence to look for:
- Track record of collecting relevant data types
- Partnerships with data providers
- Data infrastructure investments
- Mentions of data assets or capabilities
- Industry norms for data availability
- Data maturity assessment

**Scoring:** High if data likely available and accessible, Medium if some gaps, Low if significant data gaps

---

### 8. Quick Win Potential
**Can meaningful early results be demonstrated within 3-6 months?**

Considerations:
- Availability of training data and labels
- Complexity of the technical challenge
- Clarity of success metrics and baseline
- Organizational readiness for implementation
- Dependencies on other initiatives
- Prior similar successes in the organization

**Scoring:** High if POC-friendly with clear 3-6 month results, Medium if longer timeline, Low if very long timeline

## Use Case Prioritization Matrix

Create a 2x2 matrix to prioritize use cases:

**X-Axis:** Impact Potential (Low to High)
**Y-Axis:** Execution Feasibility (Low to High)

**Quadrant 1 (High Impact, High Feasibility) - PURSUE FIRST**
- These are the ideal starting points
- Strong business case with achievable execution
- High probability of success and meaningful impact

**Quadrant 2 (High Impact, Low Feasibility) - BUILDING BLOCKS**
- Important strategic opportunities
- May require capability building first
- Consider as multi-phase initiatives

**Quadrant 3 (Low Impact, Low Feasibility) - AVOID**
- Not worth pursuing
- Neither attractive nor achievable
- Focus resources elsewhere

**Quadrant 4 (Low Impact, High Feasibility) - OPTIONAL**
- Easy wins with limited value
- Can be pursued after higher-impact items
- Useful for building team experience and confidence

## Cross-Use Case Dependencies

Some use cases build on each other:

- **Foundation Use Cases:** Document Processing, Data Quality improvements often enable other use cases
- **Data-Heavy Use Cases:** Fraud Detection, Predictive Maintenance require mature data infrastructure
- **Customer-Facing:** Personalization, Customer Service often have cross-selling opportunities
- **Operational:** Supply Chain, Maintenance, Quality typically benefit from shared data platforms
- **Strategic:** Multiple use cases together create ecosystem advantages

Consider these dependencies when sequencing implementation across the organization.
